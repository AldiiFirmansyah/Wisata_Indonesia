Tales of Labuan Bajo

Labuan Bajo is a small fishing village located at the western tip of Flores Island, East Musa Tenggara, Indonesia. In the past, Labuan Bajo was just a simple village where most of the residents made their living as fisherman. Howeber, over time, this place has rapidly developed into one of Indonesia's top tourist destination due to it's stunning natural beauty

Tales of Jam Gadang

Jam Gadang is a clock tower built in 1926 in Bukittinggi, West Sumatra, as a gift from the Queen of the Netherlands. It was designed by Yazid Abidin and constructed by Haji Moran, featuring a clock mechanism made in Germany, the same as that of Big Ben in London. The tower’s architectural style initially reflected Dutch influence, later changed to a pagoda-style roof during the Japanese occupation, and finally adopted the traditional Minangkabau gonjong roof after Indonesia’s independence. Today, Jam Gadang stands as an iconic landmark and a major tourist attraction in Bukittinggi.

Tales of Banda Neira

Banda Neira, part of the Banda Islands in Maluku, was once the center of the global spice trade, especially for nutmeg, contested by European powers like the Portuguese, Dutch, and British. The Dutch eventually took control in the 17th century, building forts to dominate the trade. The island also served as an exile location for Indonesian nationalist figures like Mohammad Hatta and Sutan Sjahrir. Today, Banda Neira is renowned for its rich history and stunning underwater scenery.

Tales of Nusa Penida

Nusa Penida, an island southeast of Bali, was once used as a place of exile for lawbreakers. Today, it is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, including breathtaking beaches and dramatic cliffs, and has become a popular tourist destination with captivating underwater scenery.

Tales of Raja Ampat

Raja Ampat, an archipelago in West Papua, Indonesia, is renowned for its incredible marine biodiversity. Historically, it was home to indigenous Papuan tribes and a center for spice trade in the past. In recent decades, Raja Ampat has become a renowned tourist destination, attracting visitors worldwide with its natural beauty and rich underwater diversity.
